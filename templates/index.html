Interview Readiness

Key talking points for Data Scientist / Data Analyst interviews (7–10 LPA)

Business Problem

Gold is a safe-haven asset. Investors and portfolio managers need accurate price forecasts to hedge inflation risk and optimise asset allocation. By modelling GLD (SPDR Gold Shares ETF) using equity (SPX), oil (USO), and silver (SLV) indices, we capture cross-asset relationships that drive gold demand.

Why These Models?
  • Lasso — Baseline regularised linear model. Handles multicollinearity; interpretable coefficients for stakeholders.
  • Random Forest — Ensemble of trees; robust to outliers, captures non-linear patterns, provides feature importance.
  • XGBoost — Gradient-boosted trees; industry standard for tabular data, excellent generalisation.
Why Preprocess?
  • StandardScaler — Lasso penalises magnitude; unscaled features would bias regularisation toward smaller-range columns.
  • Train-test split (80/20) — Prevents data leakage; simulates unseen production data.
  • Outlier treatment — Extreme values skew linear models; identified via IQR and boxplots.
Key EDA Findings
  • SLV ↔ GLD correlation = 0.87 — Silver and gold move almost in lockstep (precious metals sector).
  • SPX ↔ GLD correlation ≈ 0.05 — Near-zero: equities and gold are uncorrelated, confirming gold's safe-haven role.
  • EUR/USD excluded from predictions — Low direct correlation with GLD; removing it reduces overfitting risk.
Model Comparison Conclusions

Random Forest achieved R² = {{ (results['Random Forest']['r2']*100)|round(2) }}% vs Lasso's {{ (results['Lasso']['r2']*100)|round(2) }}%, demonstrating that gold pricing has non-linear dynamics that tree-based ensembles capture far better than regularised linear models. XGBoost ({{ (results['XGBoost']['r2']*100)|round(2) }}%) comes close — in production the two would be ensembled for robustness. GridSearchCV confirmed optimal RF hyperparameters, and SLV consistently emerged as the most important predictor in both tree models (importance > 0.80), aligning with domain knowledge.
